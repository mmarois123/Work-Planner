### Kant on Education and Moral Formation

Kant was critical of education systems that rely heavily on external rewards. Such systems may produce obedient children but not necessarily morally strong adults.

His view: education should help children understand *why* something is right, not just condition them to follow rules for incentives. The goal is stable moral character — doing the right thing even without supervision or reward.

Relevant today: debates around parenting and education still wrestle with whether reward systems teach genuine responsibility or create dependency on incentives.

Core warning: morality built only on rewards and punishments may collapse when those systems fail. The alternative is internal discipline — choosing good behavior because you understand its value, not because you expect something in return.
